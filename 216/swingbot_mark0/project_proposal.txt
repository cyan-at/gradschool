Abstractâ€” Control of wire-borne underactuated brachiating
robots requires a robust feedback control design that can deal
with dynamic uncertainties, actuator constraints and unmeasurable states. In this paper, we develop a robust feedback control
for brachiating on flexible cables, building on previous work on
optimal trajectory generation and time-varying LQR controller
design. We propose a novel simplified model for approximation
of the flexible cable dynamics, which enables inclusion of parametric model uncertainties in the system. We then use semidefinite programming (SDP) and sum-of-squares (SOS) optimization to synthesize a time-varying feedback control with formal
robustness guarantees to account for model uncertainties and
unmeasurable states in the system. Through simulation, hardware experiments and comparison with a time-varying LQR
controller, it is shown that the proposed robust controller results
in relatively large robust backward reachable sets and is able to
reliably track a pre-generated optimal trajectory and achieve
the desired brachiating motion in the presence of parametric
model uncertainties, actuator limits, and unobservable states.
